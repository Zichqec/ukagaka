My history as a ghost developer, from April of 2020 to May of 2025.

Up until this point, the only place I've been consistent about posting announcements about new releases and updates has been the Ukagaka Dream Team Discord server. I used to also post them on Tumblr sometimes, but that fell by the wayside pretty quickly.

However, I am keenly aware that Discord is not a good place for all of my history to be archived. I want it on the web outside of those closed walls, in some form. So, this post is to catalogue the dates and times (when possible) that I released things, and for as long as Discord and the UDT server remain online, link to those posts.

The Tumblr posts I used to make may also be of some interest, but honestly I can't be bothered to link them up right now. They often have different timestamps than the Discord posts because I would queue them, so it's kind of a pain. Maybe one day I'll update this though. If you really want to have a look, here's a chrono tag to go through. Just be warned it's quite old.

There may be imperfections in this data. There are so many entries, it's very possible I've copied something improperly. If you notice something is off, please do tell me and I'll correct it. However, I won't be surprised if there are always small flaws present. In fact, there are multiple items that I wasn't able to turn up a release post or update posts for, and so they're just missing from this data. It's possible I never announced them at all, because back in 2020-2021 I wasn't as rigorous about recording everything. But, I've tried my best to represent everything I can.

I will also make one other note. While this history starts in April of 2020, that was just the first time I released a ghost. For at least a month or two before then I had been working on S the Skeleton, and I've been tinkering with ghost development as far back as 2016. It's just that none of the stuff I worked on back then ever saw the light of day, and so is not recorded here.

So while this is a 5 year history, it does not represent when my interest in ukagaka began, or when I first started looking into how to make my own.
